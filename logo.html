Rationale

The 7th Hell logo uses bold red lettering, a dominant “7,” and an integrated handgun silhouette to capture the game’s core identity: a descent from ordinary school life into escalating chaos. The small, black “th” hints at Kim’s lost innocence as her first‑day ID photo spirals into a nightmare, while the aggressive red “HELL” reflects the infection spreading through the school and the rising danger on each floor. The gun shape reinforces the shift from student life to survival, mirroring the side‑scrolling combat that defines the gameplay. Altogether, the logo visually echoes the narrative structure, each level darker, more distorted, and more hostile, culminating in the seventh floor, where Kim confronts Judson and the full weight of the descent comes together.

Shapes & Logo Font Choice

The shapes and font choices in the 7th Hell logo lean into sharp, geometric forms and heavy, block‑like typography to reflect the game’s descent‑driven structure and escalating tension. The dominant numerals and letters use rigid, angular silhouettes that evoke the feeling of reinforced metal or warning signage, fitting for a school turning into a hostile, fortified maze. The smaller, lighter “th” contrasts this with a more neutral, almost academic feel, hinting at the ordinary school life that’s being swallowed by chaos. The integrated handgun silhouette adds a stark, linear shape that cuts through the composition, reinforcing the shift from safety to survival. Altogether, the geometric weight, bold typeface, and weapon‑like contours create a visual identity that mirrors the game’s tone: structured, oppressive, and increasingly dangerous as Kim and Meryl fight their way downward.
